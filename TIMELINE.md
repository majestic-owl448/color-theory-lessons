# Course Timeline: Color Theory for Digital Design

This timeline outlines the progression of topics, lessons, and hands-on milestones in the Color Theory course.

---

## Unit 1: Seeing and Describing Color
*Build your visual vocabulary for color.*

- **Lesson 1.1: What Color Does in Interface Design** — Introduction to the functional roles of color in UI.
- **Lesson 1.2: Hue, Saturation, and Lightness** — Mastering the three core dimensions of color.
- **Lesson 1.3: Contrast and Readability** — Understanding how lightness difference creates legibility.
- **Lesson 1.4: Warm and Cool Colors in Practice** — Using color temperature to set mood and hierarchy.
- **Lesson 1.5: Visual Hierarchy Through Color** — Guiding the user's eye using saturation and value.
- **Lesson 1.6: Basic Color Relationships and Harmony** — Monochromatic, analogous, and complementary schemes.
- **Milestone 1: Read the Interface** — Analyze a real interface mockup using your new visual vocabulary.

---

## Unit 2: How Screens Make Color
*Understand light-based and pigment-based color models.*

- **Lesson 2.1: Two Ways Color Mixes** — The fundamental difference between light (additive) and ink (subtractive).
- **Lesson 2.2: How RGB Light Works** — Deep dive into the Red, Green, and Blue channels of your screen.
- **Lesson 2.3: Subtractive Color for Digital Designers** — Why print colors look different and how CMYK works.
- **Lesson 2.4: Seeing Pixels as Light, Not Paint** — Shifting your mental model from physical pigments to digital light.
- **Lesson 2.5: Applying Additive Thinking to UI Design** — Practical tips for mixing colors on screen.
- **Milestone 2: Mix for Screen** — Prove you can classify color models and reason about RGB channels.

---

## Unit 3: Digital Color in Programming
*Master HEX, RGB, HSL, and alpha in code.*

- **Lesson 3.1: Why Digital Design Needs Color Formats** — Understanding the bridge between design tools and code.
- **Lesson 3.2: HEX and RGB** — Reading and writing base-16 and decimal color values.
- **Lesson 3.3: HSL and HSLA** — Why HSL is often the preferred format for designers who code.
- **Lesson 3.4: Alpha, Transparency, and Layered Color** — Working with opacity and background blending.
- **Lesson 3.5: Gradients, CSS Color Usage, and Theme Building** — Implementing complex color transitions in CSS.
- **Lesson 3.6: Design Tokens and Role-Based Color Systems** — Moving from hardcoded values to semantic variables.
- **Milestone 3: Build a UI Palette in Code** — Construct a coherent digital color system using implementation-style thinking.

---

## Unit 4: Human Vision and Color Perception
*Understand how the visual system constructs color and how perception varies.*

- **Lesson 4.1: How Humans Perceive Color** — The biology of rods, cones, and brain-based color processing.
- **Lesson 4.2: Types of Color Vision Deficiency** — Understanding Protan, Deutan, and Tritan variations.
- **Lesson 4.3: Seeing Through Simulated Eyes** — Using tools to experience interfaces as others do.
- **Lesson 4.4: What Color Perception Means for Design** — Why you cannot rely on color alone to communicate meaning.
- **Milestone 4: Design Beyond Your Own Eyes** — Identify fragile interface patterns that fail users with different perception.

---

## Unit 5: Accessible Color in Practice
*Apply WCAG contrast and color guidance to real interfaces.*

- **Lesson 5.1: Text Contrast in Practice** — Mastering the 4.5:1 and 3:1 WCAG thresholds for readability.
- **Lesson 5.2: Non-Text Contrast for Controls and Graphics** — Ensuring buttons, icons, and states are visible.
- **Lesson 5.3: Color-Only Problems and Redundant Cues** — Adding icons, patterns, and text to support color signals.
- **Lesson 5.4: Accessible Patterns for Real Interfaces** — Repairing common UI components like alerts, tabs, and forms.
- **Lesson 5.5: Accessibility Audit Workflow** — A systematic approach to reviewing a design for color compliance.
- **Lesson 5.6: Inclusive Testing and Review** — Integrating accessibility into your regular design process.
- **Milestone 5: Accessibility Rescue** — Audit and repair a flawed interface for color accessibility.

---

## Unit 6: Color Systems and Advanced Topics
*Apply color strategically in modern digital design.*

- **Lesson 6.1: From Individual Colors to Color Systems** — Scaling color decisions across large products.
- **Lesson 6.2: Building Semantic Color Roles for UI** — Defining roles like 'surface', 'on-surface', and 'primary'.
- **Lesson 6.3: Brand Constraints and Hierarchy** — Balancing brand identity with usability and accessibility.
- **Lesson 6.4: Dark Mode and Theme Pairing** — Adapting palettes for light and dark environments.
- **Lesson 6.5: Color for Charts and Data Visualization** — Categorical vs. sequential palettes in data design.
- **Lesson 6.6: Color Spaces and Modern Screens** — Understanding sRGB, P3, and wide-gamut displays.
- **Lesson 6.7: Final System Review and Stress Test** — How to validate a color system before it ships.
- **Milestone 6: Color System Capstone** — Demonstrate mastery across all course topics in a final comprehensive challenge.
